Probiotic Multi Enzymes: Can they Be Beneficial For Weight Loss?

Peter Potylicki 03.08.2024
(Updated on 03.08.2024 )

Probiotic multi-enzymes are dietary supplements that combine helpful probiotic strains with digestive enzymes to support good digestion, nutrient absorption, and overall digestive health. These supplements use the teamwork of probiotics, which help keep a healthy balance of gut bacteria, and digestive enzymes, which break down carbs, proteins, and fats into absorbable parts. This combo aims to address both the microbial balance and the enzymatic breakdown of food, offering full support for digestive wellbeing.


The importance of probiotics and digestive enzymes is well-known. Probiotics like Lactobacillus and Bifidobacterium contribute to a balanced gut microbiota, immune function, and the relief of digestive disorders like irritable bowel syndrome (IBS) and inflammatory bowel disease (IBD). Digestive enzymes like amylase, protease, and lipase play key roles in breaking down food into smaller molecules, reducing digestive discomfort, and enhancing nutrient absorption.

Together, these parts of probiotic multi-enzymes ensure better digestion, reduced bloating, and improved nutrient uptake. Probiotic multi-enzymes have also gotten attention for their potential benefits in weight management.

Probiotics and Enzymes for Weight Loss

Probiotics and enzymes may help with weight loss in a few ways:

  • They can make you feel fuller by changing gut hormones. This means you eat less.
  • They help your body absorb nutrients better.
  • They lower inflammation and help with metabolism.

Some studies show certain probiotics can impact fat absorption. This is a more natural way to lose weight compared to other supplements like thermogenics. Those can have side effects.

But more research is still needed to know if probiotics and enzymes really help with weight loss. Some studies have not shown clear results for lowering weight or BMI.

Still, these supplements are good for digestive health, immunity, and metabolism.

If trying probiotics and enzymes, know:

  • Proper dosage matters.
  • They may interact with medications.
  • Choose high-quality brands that are third-party tested. Check the ingredients.

What's in Probiotic Digestive Enzymes?

Probiotic digestive enzymes are supplements that combine helpful probiotics and enzymes. They aim to improve digestion and nutrient absorption.

Probiotics

Probiotics are live microbes that are good for your health, especially your digestive system[1][2]. They help keep your gut bacteria balanced, which helps digestion and nutrient absorption.

  • Lactobacillus: This bacteria is found in foods like yogurt and kefir. It helps break down lactose and sugars, keeping your gut bacteria balanced[3][4].
  • Bifidobacterium: This common probiotic makes vitamins and fatty acids that are good for gut health[3][4]. These strains help your gut work well, support your immune system, and ease digestive problem symptoms like IBS and IBD[1][2].

Digestive Enzymes

Digestive enzymes are proteins that make chemical reactions happen. They break down carbs, proteins, and fats.

Amylase: Breaks down carbs into simple sugars.

Protease: Breaks down proteins into amino acids.

Lipase: Breaks down fats into fatty acids and glycerol.

Cellulase: Breaks down cellulose, a complex carb found in plant cell walls.

These enzymes make sure food breaks down into smaller pieces that are easier to absorb. This helps avoid digestive problems and helps your body take in nutrients better.

How Probiotics and Enzymes Work Together

Taking probiotics and enzymes together gives you better digestion and nutrient absorption. Here's how:

  • Probiotics keep your gut bacteria balanced. This is key for digestion and immunity.
  • Enzymes help break down carbs, proteins, and fats. This makes them easier to take in.
  • Together, they improve your gut balance AND food breakdown. This supports overall digestive health.

People with digestive problems can take probiotic enzymes to:

  • Better absorb nutrients
  • Reduce digestive symptoms
  • Support a balanced gut

The human gut actually has a buncha different microbes livin' in it - we're talkin' bacteria, archaea, viruses, fungi, protozoa. This whole mix of bugs is called the gut microbiota, microbiome or intestinal microflora. These little guys play a big role in keeping us healthy...or making us sick!

Probiotics - the good bacteria and yeasts - usually do their thing in our guts by messing with the microbiota. Whether the probiotics can set up shop in our gut lining depends on what bacteria we already got, the specific probiotic strain, and where in the GI tract they're trying to move in.

Our microbiota is clutch for getting nutrients and energy from food. Studies with mice say the gut bugs not only change how we use food energy, but they even affect how much energy we burn and store in our bodies. We gotta see if that holds up in humans too tho.

Probiotics can tweak these energy processes in a few ways: They can ramp up bile salt production which uses more bile acids so we need more (lowering cholesterol). They can latch onto cholesterol in the small intestine to block absorption. And they can take in and change cholesterol to make it part of the bacteria.

Also, lactobacilli and bifidobacteria make short-chain fatty acids. These lower cholesterol made by the liver and help control cholesterol.

During digestion, enzymes like amylase, protease, and lipase break down carbs, proteins, and fats into smaller pieces that the body can absorb.

Probiotics work with these enzymes to keep good bacteria balanced in the gut. This is key for good digestion and health.

Benefits for Weight Loss

The possible benefits of probiotic multi-enzymes for losing weight are supported by several ways these supplements may help with weight management.

Regulation of Appetite

Probiotic multi-enzymes may help control appetite by affecting gut hormones responsible for hunger and feeling full. Research suggests that a balanced gut microbiota can improve signaling of these hormones, potentially reducing overeating and promoting weight loss[6]. This regulation of appetite is crucial for people trying to manage their calorie intake more effectively.

Enhanced Nutrient Absorption

Efficient digestion helped by digestive enzymes can lead to better nutrient absorption, which is essential for overall health and metabolism. Proper absorption of nutrients like vitamins and minerals can prevent deficiencies that may contribute to weight gain[6]. By ensuring the body receives and uses essential nutrients effectively, these supplements can support metabolic processes and energy production.

Reduced Inflammation

Several enzymes found in probiotic multi-enzyme supplements, such as bromelain and papain, have been shown to have anti-inflammatory properties. By breaking down proteins that contribute to inflammation, these enzymes can support healing and reduce chronic inflammation[13]. Additionally, probiotics can promote growth of beneficial gut bacteria, further regulating the immune system and reducing inflammation[13]. Reduced inflammation is associated with lower risks of chronic diseases and may support weight management efforts.

Support for Irritable Bowel Syndrome (IBS)

Probiotic multi-enzyme supplements have been found to reduce symptoms of Irritable Bowel Syndrome (IBS), a condition that can significantly impact quality of life. By improving digestive health and reducing symptoms like bloating and abdominal pain, these supplements can make it easier for people with IBS to maintain a healthy weight[13].

Reduced Fat Absorption

Research suggests probiotics may help reduce the amount of dietary fat absorbed by the body. A study in the Journal of Functional Foods found people who took a probiotic supplement for six weeks had a significant reduction in body weight and BMI compared to a placebo group[13]. The researchers also saw a decrease in absorption of dietary fat, indicating probiotics may be effective in promoting weight loss and reducing obesity-related disease risk[13].

Impact on Metabolism

Probiotics can play a role in regulating metabolism, helping burn calories more efficiently. Certain probiotic strains, like those from Lactobacillus and Bifidobacterium groups, have been shown to assist with weight and fat loss by increasing metabolism and balancing gut microbiota[14]. Regular intake of these probiotics over at least 12 weeks is recommended to achieve noticeable weight loss benefits[14].

Clinical Studies and Research

The goal of some clinical studies has been to look at the weight-losing effects of different doses of probiotic diet supplements. The studies also wanted to see how safe the supplements were and how well they were tolerated. One important study was a 3-month double-blinded, randomized, placebo-controlled trial. It was followed by a 3-month open phase. The study was done at Karolinska Institutet in Sweden. In this study, the probiotic AB001 was tested at single, double, and triple doses. It was compared to a placebo. The main outcome was the change in weight. Other outcomes included changes in BMI, waist size, blood pressure, blood fats, glucose metabolism, liver enzymes, vitamin levels, and bowel habits. The trial included 81 volunteers aged 18-45 years with overweight. After 3 months, no big differences were seen between the groups in terms of weight, BMI, waist size, blood pressure, or biomarkers[15].

Limited Effectiveness

Probiotics have shown limited ability to decrease body weight and BMI. A meta-analysis of randomized controlled trials (RCTs) found probiotics were not effective for weight loss. The analysis highlighted the need for more well-designed RCTs. These are needed to look at the effect of probiotics on body weight in more detail[16]. Another systematic review and meta-analysis focused on how well probiotics helped patients with overweight or obesity lose weight. The patients also had related metabolic diseases. The study found no major decrease in body weight. However, there was a significant decrease in BMI, waist size, and hip size[17].

Antibiotic-Associated Diarrhea

Studies have also looked at the effects of specific probiotic strains on antibiotic-associated diarrhea (AAD). For example, a systematic review and meta-analysis of 12 RCTs involved Lactobacillus rhamnosus GG (LGG). It showed a reduction in the risk of AAD from 22.4% to 12.3% in patients treated with antibiotics. This effect was statistically significant in children but not adults. Another systematic review and meta-analysis of 21 RCTs involved Saccharomyces boulardii. It indicated a reduction in AAD risk from 17.4% to 8.2% in adults. In children, the risk went from 20.9% to 8.8%[8][9].

How Much to Take and When for Probiotic Digestive Enzymes

General Tips

For general digestive health, take 1 to 10 billion CFUs (colony forming units) a day. But people with digestive issues or who want specific benefits may need more. Experts say to start low and go up slowly if needed to avoid side effects like gas and bloating.

Tailor the Amount

The right probiotic enzyme dose depends on the person. Things like age, health, and medical conditions matter. For example, people with digestive problems may need more for a while.

Timing and Type

Timing is also key for probiotics to work well. Take them away from meals so the good bacteria survive better. For capsules, check if you can open them to start with half a dose.

Specific Strains

Products with more strains and higher CFUs, like 20 billion+, may work better. But the strains should be proven. Like, De111 is clinically effective and guaranteed alive, so it's likely to work.

Interactions and Side Effects

  • Probiotic supplements with enzymes are usually safe. But they can interact with some medications or other supplements. It's really important to talk to your doctor if you take prescription meds like immunosuppressants or antibiotics.
  • Some people might get side effects like more bloating or constipation. You should stop taking the supplement and see your healthcare provider if you have bad or ongoing issues.

Final Considerations

  • Always check the expiration date to make sure the supplement isn't expired. Expired products might not work or could be harmful.
  • It's good to start with a lower dose and slowly increase to find the right amount for you.
  • If you have any serious side effects, stop taking it and get medical advice right away.

Comparing Probiotic Multi-Enzyme Supplements to Other Weight Loss Supplements

Lots of folks use supplements to help them lose weight. Probiotic multi-enzyme supplements are getting popular, but how do they stack up against other weight loss supplements? Let's compare.

Probiotics

Probiotics, especially certain strains like Lactobacillus gasseri and Lactobacillus sakei, seem promising for weight loss. Studies show they can really reduce body weight, BMI, and fat percentage for people taking them. Probiotics help your digestion work right, prevent bloating and constipation, and may even reduce how much fat your body absorbs. That all supports losing weight! But probiotic supplements usually just have one strain. Probiotic multi-enzyme supplements give you probiotics plus digestive enzymes, so they take a more complete approach.

Digestive Enzymes

Digestive enzyme supplements help your body break down food into nutrients. This is great if you don't make enough enzymes on your own. They have enzymes like proteases, amylases, and lipases to improve digestion and nutrient absorption. While not sold as weight loss supplements, better digestion gives you more energy to be active and follow a healthy lifestyle. Both are key for losing weight!

Other Supplements

Some other common weight loss supplements are thermogenics, appetite suppressants, and fat blockers. Thermogenics like caffeine increase metabolism and calorie burn. Appetite suppressants reduce hunger so you eat less. Fat blockers stop your body from absorbing dietary fats, lowering calorie intake. But these can have side effects like jitteriness, digestion issues, and more.

The Verdict

Probiotic multi-enzyme supplements take a safer, more natural approach compared to other weight loss supplements. Thermogenics and appetite suppressants may work fast, but often at the cost of your health. Probiotics and enzymes enhance your body's natural processes for more sustainable, long-term weight loss. They also improve gut health, which is tied to overall wellbeing.

So if you're looking to lose weight with supplements, probiotic multi-enzymes are a solid option to consider! They likely have fewer risks than other supplements while still providing real benefits. Just be sure to talk to your doctor before starting any new supplement regimen.

Consumer Stuff to Think About When Picking Probiotics and Enzymes for Weight Loss

Check for Third-Party Testing

  • It's good to pick stuff that's been tested for bad stuff by an outside lab that follows high standards. This makes sure the supplements are pure and strong.
  • Good companies will show you a Certificate of Analysis after the lab tests. This proves they care about being open and keeping you safe.

Choose Brands You Can Trust

  • Go with brands that are known for doing the right thing and following industry best practices.
  • Make sure they follow manufacturing rules from the FDA.
  • Also check that their facilities follow FDA regulations so the products do what they claim.

Look at the Ingredients

  • The probiotics should list the specific strains, names, and amounts. This helps you understand the possible benefits of each one.
  • Make sure the ingredients are safe and match up with science.

Check the Storage and Directions

  • Some supplements need special storage like the fridge to keep working well.
  • Clear labels with serving sizes are important to take the right amount.

Watch Out for Interactions and Side Effects

  • Probiotics may interact with other meds or supplements. Talk to your doctor first, especially if you have health conditions or take medications.
  • Probiotics are generally safe but may cause temporary stomach issues or rarely allergic reactions.

Consider the Price

  • Prices vary a lot. Dollar signs give you an idea of cost.
  • More expensive doesn't always mean better. Find the balance between cost, quality, and what works for your budget.

By thinking about these things, you can make a good choice to support your weight loss and digestive health!
